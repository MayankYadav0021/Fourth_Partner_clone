Answering some common questions on On-Site Solar Solutions

There are two main financing models: CAPEX and OPEX. In the CAPEX model, the client owns the solar system by paying upfront. In the OPEX model, the developer installs, owns, and maintains the system, while the client pays only for the energy consumed through a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA).

Businesses can save between 30% and 50% on electricity bills, depending on their energy consumption patterns and local energy policies.

Most solar power systems have an operational lifespan of 25 to 30 years, provided they are properly maintained.

System performance can be monitored using SCADA platforms, real-time dashboards, and mobile-based monitoring applications.

Available solutions include Rooftop Solar, Ground-Mounted Systems, Building-Integrated Photovoltaics (BIPV), Solar Carports, and Floating Solar installations.

Key considerations include financial stability, project execution experience, long-term O&M (Operations & Maintenance) support, and technological innovation.

RE developers accelerate clean energy adoption by delivering end-to-end solar solutions to enterprises, helping reduce carbon emissions and meet climate targets.
